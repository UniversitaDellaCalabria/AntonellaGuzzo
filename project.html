R&D Projects

Title:Scientific Management and Responsibility of Projects Funded by Competitive Calls

POR 2025:Serves as the scientific lead of the project “IAxPMI — Artificial Intelligence for Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs),” funded by the Calabria Region (POR call “Support for research, development and innovation projects, including collaboration with research organizations, in the S3 priority areas and trajectories”). The project aims to develop a platform offering AI-powered advanced services for SMEs, including management control tools, intelligent financial alerts, and credit score–based classification. Project partners (in addition to the University of Calabria) are Altilia (lead partner) and EVO-BI.

PON MIMIT 2025: Serves as the scientific lead of the DIMES research unit for the project “PREDICI,” funded under the PON MIMIT “Entrepreneurial Discovery” program (Sustainable Growth Fund, PN RIC 2021–2027). The project develops machine-learning and automated-reasoning technologies using financial and non-financial data from companies and public bodies. Partners include REVELIS (lead), EVO-BI, and the University of Calabria.

COST 2023: Serves as the scientific lead of the University of Calabria’s research unit and Italy’s representative on the Management Committee for the European COST Action CA22104, “Behavioral Next Generation in Wireless Networks for Cyber Security (BEiNG-WISE).”

PRIN 2022: Serves as the scientific lead of the University of Calabria’s research unit for the PRIN 2022 project “G.A.N.D.A.L.F — GAN Approaches for Non-IID Aiding Learning in Federation.” The project addresses the non-IID data challenge in federated learning within the broader Edge-AI context by leveraging GAN-based machine/deep-learning methods. Partners include the University of Naples Federico II (lead) and the University of Messina.

PON MISE 2021: Served as the scientific lead for OR1 in the SOLIDO project (“Efficient solutions for the design and management of a distribution center”), funded by the 2021 PON MISE call. The project developed advanced methods to optimize warehouse storage and goods distribution. She led OR1 on machine-learning tools.
